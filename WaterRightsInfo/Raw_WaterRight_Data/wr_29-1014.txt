     The use of water under this claim is for the creation and maintenance of
     marshlands for waterfowl habitat within the Bear River Migratory Bird Refuge.
     Incidental storage of water behind shallow dikes is associated with such use.
     The Place of Use (service area) described under this claim is the entire land
     area within the boundaries of the bird refuge.  The effective service area is
     the acreage shown on the hydrographic survey maps and represents the historic
     extent of this right.  The effective service area is 43,742.74 acres.  This
     claim is limited to the sole supply acreage of 43,742.74 acres.  The total
     annual diversion limitation under this claim is 425,771 acre-feet.  The annual
     diversion limitation is based on yearly water use requirements reported for
     the refuge and the flow rate of this claim. The water demand to maintain
     optimal marshland conditions for the Bear River Migratory Bird Refuge was
     determined by comparing two hydrologic models; one  by the Utah Division of
     Water Resources and the other by the USA Fish & Wildlife Service.  The water
     volume requirements to maintain waterfowl marshlands used in the models were
     reported in the Utah Division of Fish & Game publication No. 69-12
     (Christensen, J.E., Water Requirements of Waterfowl Marshlands in Northern
     Utah). The monthly demand pattern for this claim was derived from the models
     and the flow rate of this claim.  The monthly demand pattern for this claim is
     as follows:

     This amount is limited to that diverted for beneficial use and is the maximum
     amount of water available from the described diversion points and all natural
     sources.  The 4 points of diversion described in this claim are referenced by
     Certificate of Appropriation No. a686. These points are not mapped on the
     hydrographic survey map.  The State Engineer has re-mapped the points of
     diversion.  Five points of diversion are shown on the hydrographic survey map
     corresponding to those originally described under Certificate No. a686. They
     are listed and described below.  Points 1 & 2 correspond respectively with the
     diversions on the Bear River at the Reeder Overflow and the Whistler Canal.
     Point 3 corresponds with the originally described diversion on the Bear River
     near the old Duckville Headquarters.  Points 4 - 6 correspond with the
     originally described diversion near the old Refuge Headquarters.  Points 4 - 6
     describe each of the diversions into the three main channels controlled by
     radial gates. POINTS OF DIVERSION SHOWN ON HYDROGRAPHIC SURVEY MAP #1) S 1856
     ft. W 2886 ft. from the NE Corner of Section 18, T9N, R2W, SLB&M. Source:
     Bear River at Reeder Overflow #2) N 220 ft. E 446 ft. from the SW Corner of

     ft. W 325 ft. from the NE Corner of Section 35, T9N, R4W, SLB&M. Source: Bear
     River near old Duckville Headquarters #4) S 154 ft. E 2341 ft. from the NW
     Corner of Section 35, T9N, R3W, SLB&M.Source: Bear River near old Headquarters
     #5) S 207 ft. E 2270 ft. from the NW Corner of Section 35, T9N, R4W, SLB&M.
     Source: Bear River near old Headquarters #6) S 335 ft. E 2309 ft. from the NW
     Corner of Section 35, T9N, R4W, SLB&M. Source: Bear River near old
     Headquarters The State Engineer recognizes both groups of descriptions as
     valid points of diversion. The total acreage represented on this claim
     reflects that mapped by the State Engineer`s office.  It is noted, however,
     that the FWS has also mapped the effective service area within the Refuge
     boundary and has calculated an acreage of 44,325 acres.  This represents a
     difference of 582.26 acres (1.3%).  Both the State Engineer and the FWS
     believe the difference in acreage is due to the different methods of mapping
     by the respective agencies.  The State Engineer and the FWS accept this
     difference and agree this claim  represents the current service area under WUC
     29-1014.

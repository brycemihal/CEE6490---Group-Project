<B><FONT COLOR='green'>OTHER COMMENTS**********************************************************************************************************************</FONT></B>
<B><FONT COLOR='green'>====================================================================================================================================</FONT></B>
     Although a priority of 1869 is claimed for this right, due to the concentrat-
     ion of surface waters by the construction of the old Central Pacific Railroad
     grade, no physical diversions other than spreader ditches were made by the
     claimant or his predecessors prior to 1903. It is therefore believed that the
     claimant holds a diligence right to the use of waters as they have historical-
     ly and naturally flown over claimants property. However, the use of these
     waters is limited to the same conditions as set forth for adjacent lands owned
     and operated by claimant under Judge Lewis Jones' Decree in Civil No. 4473, in
     the First Judicial District Court in and for Box Elder County. (See attached
     Decree.) Because of the nature of this right, no call can be made on water
     covered by rights to irrigation or stockwatering established to the north and
     east and upstream from claimants lands.

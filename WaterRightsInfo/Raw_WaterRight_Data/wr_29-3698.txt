<B><FONT COLOR='green'>OTHER COMMENTS**********************************************************************************************************************</FONT></B>
<B><FONT COLOR='green'>====================================================================================================================================</FONT></B>
     The use of water under this claim is for the creation and maintenance of
     marshlands for waterfowl habitat within the Bear River Bird Refuge.
     Incidental storage of water behind shallow dikes is associated with such
     use.The Place of Use (service area) described under this claim is the entire
     land area within the boundaries of the refuge.  The effective service area is
     the acreage shown on the hydrographic survey maps and represents the historic
     extent of this right.  The effective service area is 326.60 acres.  The total
     annual diversion limitation under this claim is 2,000 acre-feet.  The annual
     diversion limitation is based on yearly water use requirements reported for
     the Bear River Migratory Bird Refuge and the flow rate of this claim.

     The water demand to maintain optimal marshland conditions for the Bear River
     Migratory Bird Refuge was determined by comparing two hydrologic models; one
     by the Utah Division of Water Resources and the other by the USA Fish &
     Wildlife Service.  The water volume requirements to maintain waterfowl
     marshlands used in the models were reported in the Utah Division of Fish &
     Game publication No. 69-12 (Christensen, J.E., Water Requirements of Waterfowl
     Marshlands in Northern Utah).

     The monthly demand pattern for this claim is specific to the Bear River Delta.
     The monthly demand pattern is as follows:
     Monthly Demand:
     Month         Diversion Limitation   AF
     January         -------
     February        -------
     March           -------
     April           -------
     May              750.00
     June             250.00
     July            -------
     August          -------
     Sept.            200.00
     October          400.00
     November         400.00
     December        -------
     Total          2,000.00 AF/year

     The State Engineer believes that these waters were historically used during
     high flows of the Bear River.  Therefore, this claim will be included in the
     priority list of the Lower Bear River distribution system and will be
     recognized during periods of high water flow generally from May 1st to June
     15th and September 15th to November 30th.

     This amount is limited to that diverted for beneficial use and is the maximum
     amount of water available from the described diversion points and all natural
     sources.

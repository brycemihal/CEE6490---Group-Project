     The use of water under this claim is for the creation and maintenance of
     marshlands for waterfowl habitat within the Bear River Migratory Bird Refuge.
     Incidental storage of water behind shallow dikes is associated with such use.
     The Place of Use (service area) described under this claim is the entire land
     area within the boundaries of the Bear River Migratory Bird Refuge.  The
     effective service area is the acreage shown on the hydrographic survey maps
     and represents the historic extent of this right.  The effective service area
     is 1,589.84 acres.  This claim is supplemental to 1014 and has no sole supply.
     The total annual diversion limitation under this claim is 3,840.80 acre-feet.
     The annual diversion limitation is based on yearly water use requirements
     reported for the Bear River Migratory Bird Refuge and the flow rate of this
     claim.

     The water demand to maintain optimal marshland conditions for the Bear River
     Migratory Bird Refuge was determined by comparing two hydrologic models; one
     by the Utah Division of Water Resources and the other by the USA Fish &
     Wildlife Service.  The water volume requirements to maintain waterfowl
     marshlands used in the models were reported in the Utah Division of Fish &
     Game publication No. 69-12 (Christensen, J.E., Water Requirements of Waterfowl
     Marshlands in Northern Utah).

     The monthly demand pattern for this claim is determined to be specific to the
